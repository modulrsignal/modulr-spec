# Intent Taxonomy

Modulr uses six intent primitives to describe the commercial reasoning capabilities of supply.

Each primitive is assigned only when the corresponding capability is the dominant organizing characteristic of the supply — not when it merely appears within it.

---

## problem_recognition

Supply organized to diagnose a specific problem.

The supply defines the problem, identifies contributing causes, and frames meaningful consequences in a way that materially supports later commercial reasoning.

Discussion of problems, complaints, or current events is insufficient. Describing a problem is not diagnosing one.

---

## comparative_evaluation

Supply in which structured comparison is the organizing structure.

The supply explicitly evaluates meaningful alternatives through evidence, tradeoffs, competing frameworks, or competing outcomes.

Simple contrasts, historical comparisons, or isolated observations do not qualify.

---

## decision_support

Supply organized to help evaluate or choose between meaningful real-world actions, products, strategies, or alternatives.

A useful test: after consuming this supply, a system could support a materially better decision about a concrete, nameable objective.

Providing useful context or general information is insufficient.

---

## execution_planning

Supply that provides an intentionally sequenced process for carrying out a real-world objective.

High-level recommendations or conceptual discussion are insufficient. The supply must organize toward execution, not simply describe it.

---

## skill_development

Supply in which intentional capability-building is the dominant organizing purpose.

The supply is structured as instruction: lessons, tutorials, frameworks, procedural teaching, or systematic knowledge progression.

Incidental explanation, background context, or teaching through reporting does not qualify.

---

## purchase_transaction_facilitation

Supply that materially supports evaluation immediately preceding a purchase or commercial transaction.

General discussion of products or markets is insufficient. The supply must be organized around the transaction evaluation itself.

---

## Notes

Primitives are not mutually exclusive. A unit of supply may carry more than one primitive when multiple dominant organizing structures are present.

An empty primitive array is a valid result. Most supply does not produce dominant commercial reasoning structures, and this is accurately represented by an empty array.

No primitive implies audience intent. All primitives describe supply capability, not what an audience member is thinking or doing.
